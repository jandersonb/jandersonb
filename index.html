About me

Originally from Auckland, New Zealand, I am currently completing a PhD at Swinburne University of Technology in Melbourne, Australia. I work with the N-body code NBODY6++GPU to simulate open star clusters. I am particularly interested in how dense stellar environments impacts populations of transient systems, such as X-ray binaries, type Ia supernovae, and compact mergers.

Projects

Some projects that I've worked on!

  • X-ray sources in Galactic open clusters
    Strong X-ray sources can indicate interacting binaries, and perhaps even an X-ray binary (accreting black hole or neutron star). We perform a multi-wavelength search for these types of objects in three Milky Way open clusters.
    Article: Incoming...
  • MOA-II data summary statistics
    MOA-II is the current and longest phase of the Microlensing Observations in Astrophysics (MOA) survey, with now over 20 years of data archived. I'm working to provide an overview of the data contained in these archives.
    Article: Incoming...
  • Observations of BlueWalker 3
    As part of a widespread observing campaign, I scheduled and performed observations of the bright LEO satellite BlueWalker 3 shortly after its deployment. We found that the satellite reached a range-corrected V magnitude of 0 mag, significantly brighter than the recommendations of the SATCON working group.
    Article: Nandakumar, S., Eggl, S., Tregloan-Reed, J., et al. including Anderson-Baldwin, J. (2023). The high optical brightness of the BlueWalker 3 satellite. Nature 623, 938-941
